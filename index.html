Journal of Fluids and Structures, 2017

Propulsive Efficiency of an Oscillating Airfoil

This research explored the aerodynamics of bio-inspired flight. Using a self-developed CFD code (UCFD) , I conducted a large-scale parametric study on an airfoil with combined pitching and heaving motions to analyze how frequency, pitch, and heave amplitudes affect propulsive efficiency.

Journal of Engineering Thermophysics, 2019

Numerical Simulation of an SCO₂ Centrifugal Compressor

This project focused on a key component for next-generation power cycles: a Supercritical CO₂ (SCO₂) centrifugal compressor. I led the aerodynamic design and performed CFD analysis to validate its performance at design and off-design conditions.

  • Key Result: Achieved a design point with 92% isentropic efficiency and a 1.21 pressure ratio.
  • Key Finding: The compressor's off-design performance curves resemble those of traditional axial compressors, confirming its operational stability.

Passion & Interests

Beyond the numbers and simulations, this is what drives me.

My Fascination with Turbomachinery

To me, turbomachinery represents the pinnacle of engineering—a perfect blend of fluid dynamics, thermodynamics, and precision manufacturing. It's the heart of our modern world, from the jet engines that connect continents to the power plants that light our cities. My career has allowed me to explore this field at two fascinating extremes: the immense scale of F-class gas turbines, where a single percentage point of efficiency can save millions, and the micro-scale of a ventricular assist device, where fluid dynamics directly translates to saving a human life. This duality—powering civilizations and sustaining individuals—is what makes me so passionate about my work. Every simulation I run and every component I design is part of this incredible story.
